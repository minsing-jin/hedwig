🦉 About Hedwig

Why Hedwig Exists

The information ecosystem is broken. YouTube optimizes for watch time. X optimizes for engagement. Both extract attention to sell ads. Neither cares whether the signals reach you actually matter to you.

Hedwig is the answer to a simple question: what if the algorithm that decides what reaches you belonged to you?

The Five Moats

  1. Socratic Onboarding — An LLM interviewer figures out what you care about through conversation, not forms.
  2. Self-Evolving Algorithm — Inspired by Karpathy's autoresearch, Hedwig experiments with its own filtering criteria, measures upvote ratios, keeps improvements, discards regressions.
  3. Boolean Feedback — Just 👍 or 👎. The system handles all the pattern interpretation.
  4. Long-Horizon Memory — Weekly preference snapshots track your taste over months, not just today.
  5. Algorithm Sovereignty — You own the fitness function. You can audit it. You can modify it. You can self-host the entire stack (MIT licensed).

What Hedwig Is NOT

  • Not another social network
  • Not an aggregator that ranks by popularity
  • Not a SaaS that hoards your data
  • Not a black box you can't inspect

Open Source

Hedwig is MIT licensed. The full source is on GitHub. You can:

  • Self-host on your Mac (the original use case)
  • Contribute new source plugins
  • Audit the evolution algorithm
  • Fork it

The hosted SaaS at hedwig.app is for users who want zero-config convenience. Self-hosting is for users who want maximum control.
